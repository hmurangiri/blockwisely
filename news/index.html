  1. Paxfull closes down operations:

    Paxful, a well-known peer-to-peer trading platform for cryptocurrency, has announced its decision to shut down its marketplace due to difficulties stemming mainly from a lawsuit filed against the company by one of its co-founders. The regulatory challenges faced by the industry, particularly in the US, have also grown, prompting the company to take the most secure option and ask users to withdraw their funds and trade elsewhere.

    Paxful's collapse comes at a time when the crypto industry is recovering from a year-long slump, although the impact on the industry is expected to be less severe than the collapse of other platforms due to users not losing any funds. Paxful had emerged as a prominent P2P trading platform for cryptocurrency in sub-Saharan Africa.

  2. Binance shops are opening all over Nairobi town:

    Binance, a popular cryptocurrency exchange, is opening physical shops in Nairobi, the capital city of Kenya. This move could indicate an effort to expand their customer base and increase accessibility to their services.

    Bitcoin, the largest cryptocurrency, surpassed $30,000 in value in April 2023, marking a new all-time high since June 2022. This comes after a period of significant growth and increasing mainstream adoption, with more institutional investors and companies showing interest in cryptocurrency.

  2. Bitcoin pizza day on May 22nd:
